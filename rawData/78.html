Flight diverted in misunderstanding over Jewish prayer ritual

  • Comments
By Douglas Stanglin, USA TODAY
Updated

A US Airway Express flight from New York was diverted to Philadelphia today following a misunderstanding over a teenager's Orthodox Jewish prayer ritual that uses small black boxes attached to leather straps.

The 17-year-old boy was using tefillin, which contain biblical passages. When used in prayer, one box is strapped to the arm while the other box is placed on the head.

"It's something that the average person is not going to see very often, if ever," FBI spokesman J.J. Klaver says.

The teen explained the ritual after being questioned by crew members, but the captain of the US Airways Express plane, which was en route to Louisville decided to land in Philadelphia anyway, the Associated Press reports.

Authorities said the White  Plains, N.Y., teen, who was traveling with his 16-year-old sister, was very cooperative.
