Federal Marshals Seize Products From Toledo Company

TOLEDO -- Federal marshals have seized several specialized beds made in Toledo that authorities are blaming for seven deaths.

The Food and Drug Administration says the enclosed beds made by Vail Products Incorporated have trapped about 30 people. The beds are designed to keep special-needs patients from falling out or wandering off. Marshals confiscated three versions of the bed Monday.

The FDA says it already warned the company twice that it wasn't meeting regulations.

Last month, the parents of a 7-year-old girl with cerebral palsy sued the company. They say their daughter suffocated in a faulty bed and that the company didn't notify them about a recall for that model.

Company officials say their beds aren't dangerous if used properly.

The Associated Press contributed to this story news@wtol.com
