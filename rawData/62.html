Venus: Overview: Planetary Hot Spot

Need-to-Know
Ten things everyone should know about our solar system's hottest planet.
Venus In Depth
Get to know this cloud-enshrouded world.
Similar in structure and size to Earth, Venus' thick, toxic atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. A permanent layer of clouds traps heat, creating surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. Glimpses below the clouds reveal volcanoes and deformed mountains. Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction of most planets.

Venus: Overview Timeline
Mariner 2 at Venus
14 Dec 1962
Mariner 2 was the first successful mission to another planet by any country. During a 42 minute scan, the spacecraft showed that the surface temperature on Venus was at least 797 degrees Fahrenheit (425 degrees Celsius) -- hot enough to melt lead. It also showed that Venus rotates in the opposite direction from most planets in our solar system.
